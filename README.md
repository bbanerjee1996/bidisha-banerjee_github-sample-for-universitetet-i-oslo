# Agrivoltaics Techno-Economic Assessment Model

Techno-economic assessment model for evaluating the long-term feasibility of a grid-connected agrivoltaics system in India.

---

## Research Context

Agrivoltaics combines solar photovoltaic generation with agricultural activities on the same land parcel. This model was developed to evaluate the long-term financial and operational feasibility of a grid-connected agrivoltaics system under varying technical, financial, and operational conditions.

The work was developed as part of broader research on renewable energy deployment, dual land-use systems, and sustainable energy transitions in India. The model integrates renewable energy generation analysis with financial assessment to understand long-term project viability.

---

## Objective

The objective of this model is to assess the techno-economic performance of a grid-connected agrivoltaics system over a 25-year project lifetime using engineering-economic analysis principles.

The model evaluates:

- Annual energy generation
- System degradation over time
- Revenue generation from electricity sales
- Agricultural co-benefits
- Debt-equity financing structure
- Loan repayment schedules
- O&M cost escalation
- Working capital requirements
- Depreciation and salvage value
- Annual cash flows
- Net Present Value (NPV)
- Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
- Payback Period

## Methodology

The model estimates annual energy generation using plant capacity, annual runtime, auxiliary losses, and generation degradation assumptions.

Annual project cash flows are evaluated by incorporating:

- Electricity revenue
- Agricultural income
- O&M escalation
- Loan repayment obligations
- Working capital interest
- Depreciation schedules
- Discounted cash flow analysis

The analysis is performed over a 25-year operational lifetime to evaluate long-term financial feasibility under varying operational conditions.
